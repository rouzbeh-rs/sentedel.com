What's new in v2.1

The NTE segment is a free-text field inside structured X12 EDI transactions where clinicians write notes — prior authorizations, referrals, clinical context. These contain PHI (patient names, dates, member IDs) in natural language, not in predictable element positions.

Industry insight

"Look at how the model fares against PHI data in the NTE segment — that's where most of these solutions lag."

— EDI de-identification professional

v2.1 adds NTE free-text support by fine-tuning a single model on both structured segments and LLM-generated NTE content (217 unique templates from Qwen 2.5 7B). No routing, no ensemble — one model handles both.

Relaxed vs strict

On 500 held-out synthetic transactions, relaxed matching finds 11,000 of 11,002 labeled elements (99.98%); strict exact-boundary recall is 91.0%.

Boundary alignment

S-tag label mapping reduced subword boundary errors, improving strict boundary recall from 0.8% (baseline) to 91.0% on the same benchmark.

Training efficiency

Dynamic sequence packing and LoRA cut training time by ~62% compared to our baseline training setup.
